The Moderates

The need for balance
Maggie Ping · Youth Politica · Posted: May 22 2023
       Especially with the rise of young conservative voters, increasingly extreme ideologies and disdain for “left wokeness,” the tenets of political parties have become diametrically opposed. They set up fortresses, shooting at every chance to elevate their irreconcilable views: Terms like “Justinflation,” “fascists,” “communism,” and “socialists” float around just to soil their targets. Insults splatter Twitter pages, weaponizing silence and provocative comments in the process of a governmental decision. It almost suggests that associating with one party means to deny the other. To deny the other, means to reject and therefore to receive the consequences of that end. But on what basis? Yes, our compulsion to act within the boundary of our “accepted” ideology, irrespective of the circumstances and conditions is tempting, but is an impossible objective.

     Take the death penalty discussion as an example; the conservative side promotes it, and liberalism condemns it. Ironically, while both sides forcefully ram their beliefs without compromise, they both base their opinions on completely separate perspectives. For criminals, conservatives may argue that death is a morally justified punishment, not considering the factuality of justice: Is death still morally justified if the criminal was wrongly charged? On the other side of the spectrum, liberals believe that the death penalty strips criminals of their universal right to life, as well as their entitlement to second chances and equal rights: But is it right to treat a child rapist, a murderer, as a regular citizen? Both ideologies often fail to consider the possibilities, pillaring all arguments within the paradigm of their party. Doesn’t striking a balance between the two seem more logical?

     Liberalism is a needed concept for inventing and discovering, individual and group rights, high aspirations, a varied paradigm of freedom, education, and growth. It gives us a framework to rid of old utterances, take youthful risks and reach new limits. Without it, we would be stuck in the past, unwilling to make transits for intellectual revolution.

     Conservatism breathes stability, preserving the “status quo.” It holds you the moment’s rest needed to make a shift into another state. Whether it’s by ensuring necessary defences, public and private administration, protecting the old, maintaining tradition and peace through disciplinary principles, conservatism brings forth the maintenance required for development.

     As like the 2 sides of a coin, their aforesaid dreams cause them to feud for dominance–one side keeps a formal view, albeit too traditionally, and the other seeks change, albeit too idealistically–but even so, they exist interconnectedly. Seeking a resolution between two ideologies is not as complicated, however. A solution is to choose the middle option, to join the growing plurality of moderates, or simply not care at all. But if you do care, why choose one when you can have the best of two worlds? Works Referenced National Post. (2022, June 21). Why Critical Race Theory Needs to be Taught in Schools.
