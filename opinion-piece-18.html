The Alaskan Willow Oil Project: a One Way Ticket to Nature’s Doom

The project is threatening the already fragile Arctic and Alaskan Lands
Fozia Kahloon · Youth Politica · Posted: May 22 2023
Oil pipelines built across Alaska (The Washington Post)     More than a million letters were written to the White House, in protest as soon as the Biden administration announced the approval of the Willow Project in Alaska. That in itself shows the great concern people share about the project. The project was started by a Houston-based energy company, ConocoPhillips. It is the largest oil project ever proposed and intends to add five drilling pads on Alaska and Arctic grounds.

     Many may be wondering how this project could be as harmful as some say, well essentially, ConocoPhillips will refreeze the tundra to drill it for oil. This would consequently, release huge carbon emissions, equivalent to the emissions released by 76 coal fired power plants yearly. Despite this, many argue that this is a positive change for citizens. Ultimately, more jobs are being created for locals in Alaska. Some communities in Alaska have agreed that this would allow more independence from the state and federal government for funding and revenue.

     However, there are greater consequences that come aligned with the approval of this project. This includes: health consequences, disregard for traditions in native communities and environmental damage. The mayor of Nuiqsut believes that the community’s health and the environment would be negatively impacted. That impact heavily outweighs the small streams of financial benefits the village would be getting. The production equipment and vehicles would release harmful pollutants that can cause cardiovascular, kidney and chronic respiratory problems for close communities. Nuiqsat has already seen a 20% increase in respiratory illnesses as oil wells have been constructed nearby. Furthermore, the mayor has spoken about the effect this has had on their sacred hunting traditions, since many animals are not found where they used to reside.

     The gravest impact of all would be to the environment. The Arctic is already on the brink of environmental catastrophe, many animals in the Arctic are facing habitat loss and are endangered. This project will increase the rate at which the Arctic meets its doom, since roads and other infrastructures have to be built before the actual pads. There will be more ice sheets melting, more habitat loss, more endangered and extinct species, higher sea levels and of course, raised temperatures.

     Seeing that this project will take years to reach the market and make profit, there is no benefit to this project. The Alaskan and Arctic lands will be deeply harmed, animals would be in grave danger and many would probably meet their extinction before the oil gets to the market. Instead of focusing on making oil more transportable and cheaper, the focus should be on being more energy efficient to help repair and mend the damages we have already inflicted on the environment.
