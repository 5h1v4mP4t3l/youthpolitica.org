Abortion Rights: Who Matters More?

Why the choice of the mother should take precedence
Fozia Kahloon · Youth Politica · Posted: May 22 2023
Women protest for abortion rights in Northern Ireland (Getty Images)      The overturning of Roe V Wade was undoubtedly a historic event. This new change meant that the legalization and access to abortion were now up to the individual states of America. It had unleashed protests, multiple debates, a Netflix documentary series, and the question: Should the mother or the fetus matter more? I, for one, believe the mother matters more.

     A study shows that 74% of women abort due to the belief that a baby would interfere with their work and education. 73% stated that they simply couldn't afford to raise a baby, and 48% stated relationship problems or fear of single motherhood. Less than 1% stated the cause to be their family’s wishes. Only 2% involve pregnancy complications. So, are the intentions right or wrong? I think it is reasonable to be concerned for yourself, as a mother, about your future. Mothers sacrifice so much for their children, and some are simply not willing to make that sacrifice yet, or at all. Not to mention, one has to consider the implications it would have on a child if the child was born when the mother was not ready. Would you rather have a child suffer in an impoverished or loveless environment? Pregnant women certainly wouldn’t want that, so the intentions behind abortion aren’t careless. They would rather have an abortion than have their child struggle throughout their childhood. The notion that women celebrate their abortion is a harmful narrative, it takes a lot to even think of getting an abortion.

      Pro-lifers believe life begins at conception. Scientifically, that’s not entirely wrong. A majority of these abortions take place before 13 weeks of gestation, which is about 93% of abortions. By the thirteenth week of pregnancy, the baby has just begun to form bones and might start moving its limbs. The baby starts developing the brain in the very early weeks of the first trimester, and by week 7 is beginning to show signs of neural activity in its developing brain. It’s clear that the “it’s just a clump of cells” argument made by pro-choicers, is simply false. However, there is no current US law that states a fetus is a living person. There is no definite agreement or law that states whether a fetus is a living being or not. Thus, no matter what opinion a person carries about the living state of a fetus, it should be up to the mother if she wants to fulfill the pregnancy or not. The fetus hasn’t been born yet and relies on the mother’s body to live. On the other hand, the mother is by law, a person entitled to the right of having complete bodily autonomy. That should include her right to abortion and her right to choose to have an abortion.
